Problem statement

Dementia is a global problem. According to various researches, the number of people with dementia will increase in the coming decades.

The first line graph shows the expected increase of dementia, illustrating that higher income countries are more likely to face an increased rate of dementia.

One of the main causes given for this increase is the aging population.

In the second line graph it is shown that Europe will also face this increased rate of dementia. As the Netherlands are also a high income country and being part of Europe, it is likely that also dementia will show an increase here.

To see if this is true, you can click for the visualisation page dementia in the Netherlands. If you want to know more about the different types of dementia, please visit the visualisation page types of dementia first.

Usage

This line graph shows prognosis based on different findings. If you hover over the lines in the graph, information about the line will be given. Hover over the dots to see the number of people with dementia in millions.
